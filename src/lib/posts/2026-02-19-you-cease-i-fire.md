> "Ceasefire according to Israel=“you cease, I fire.” Calling it “peace” is both an insult and a distraction." - Francesca Albanese in October 2025

On October 10, 2025 a ceasefire was declared between Hamas and Israel. The [20-point plan](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c70155nked7o) was developed by the Trump administration and is touted by Trump as one of the biggest successes in history. 

The ceasefire is not a success. The plan calls for an immediate end to hostilities between both parties, but before the end of October, Israeli forces had already killed more than 200 Palestinians in Gaza. [As of Dec 29](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/11/11/how-many-times-has-israel-violated-the-gaza-ceasefire-here-are-the-numbers), 2025, Israel had already killed more than 400 Palestinians and injured more than 1000.  The plan calls for full aid to be *immediately* sent to Gaza and for UN and Red Crescent aid to flow freely. However, less than half of aid trucks have been allowed to enter, with Israel delaying and blocking trucks carrying nutritional food particularly. Point 8 of the plan calls for the Rafah border crossing to be reopened. [Under the agreement](https://www.latimes.com/world-nation/story/2026-02-19/questioning-delays-palestinian-familys-grueling-return-to-gaza), 50 people should be allowed to enter and 50 medical patients should be allowed to leave to receive treatment. This is untenable, since Palestinians say there are 20,000 people seeking to leave for medical care, but the point is moot because Israeli officials are further restricting who can enter or leave, confiscating phones, money, and water bottles. 

The plan also demands Israeli troops progressively de-occupy the Strip. In the first phase, Israeli forces should pull back to the so-called "yellow line." Furthermore, the plan says, "Israel will not occupy or annex Gaza." Instead, the Israeli army has built permanent outposts and placed markers well beyond the line. The [Israeli Military](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/dec/08/yellow-line-that-divides-gaza-under-trump-plan-is-new-border-for-israel-says-military-chief) claims that the "'yellow line' is a new border line" and that "We have operational control over extensive parts of the Gaza Strip and we will remain on those defence lines." The yellow line pushes 2 million Palestinians into a coastal area smaller than Washington DC, cutting them off from half of Gaza. 

Furthermore, even if Israel followed the ceasefire and removed its occupation forces and allowed aid in Palestinians in [Gaza would not be free](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2025/11/24/no-there-is-no-ceasefire-in-gaza). Donald Trump's plan predictably puts Donald Trump in charge of the "Board of Peace" that will oversee Gaza's day-to-day operations and an "International Stabilization Force" created by the United States and its "Arab partners." Presumably the full phrase "partners in crimes against humanity" too long for Trump's attention span. The genocide over the past two years was supported and funded by the United States, and Palestinians are expected to accept US governance over Gaza. Nowhere in this "peace deal" does Israel or any of its supporters face consequences for their crimes. 

Throughout this sham, Israel turns its attention to its pre-October 7, 2023 target: the West Bank. Extremist settler attacks have continued to escalate with zero consequences from the Israeli government -- unsurprising since much of the [Israeli Knesset are settlers themselves](https://www.jpost.com/opinion/article-882026). The Israeli government not only looks the other way at the construction of illegal settlements and ethnic cleansing in the West Bank, but these are done with [government support](https://apnews.com/article/israel-palestinians-settlements-west-bank-6923448a5956ff4d90b240d871db33e6). 

The ceasefire is not a success... unless, of course, success is measured not by peace, but by giving cover to Israel as it continues its ethnic cleansing and genocide. Corporate media has parroted the US government's insistence that the "[Ceasefire is holding](https://fair.org/home/the-gaza-genocide-continues-rebranded-as-a-ceasefire/)." [Amnesty International](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2025/11/israels-genocide-against-palestinians-in-gaza-continues-unabated-despite-ceasefire/) lays out the situation clearly: "The ceasefire risks creating a dangerous illusion that life in Gaza is returning to normal. But while Israeli authorities and forces have reduced the scale of their attacks and allowed limited amounts of humanitarian aid into Gaza, the world must not be fooled. Israel’s genocide is not over."
