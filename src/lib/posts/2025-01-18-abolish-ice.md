Over the past two weekends, the Baton Rouge Democratic Socialists of America attended local protests calling for justice for Renee Good and demanding an end to the state-sponsored terrorism being doled out by the Department of Homeland Security. 

On Saturday January 10th, BRDSA and hundreds of other concerned residents gathered at an ["ICE Out For Good" protest organized by Indivisible BR](https://www.wbrz.com/news/protestors-gather-in-baton-rouge-in-response-to-fatal-ice-shooting-in-minneapolis/). BRDSA co-chair Ben W spoke to the crowd, urging people to raise their struggle beyond mere voting, and calling attention to the victories of local unions. "Voting is powerful, and you should vote -- but the other power you have is your labor! The rich people that own these companies and bribe your legislature, they can't do any of this without your labor!" Ben reminded the crowd of recent labor victories by local unions, and encouraged people to speak with their coworkers about how they can make their workplaces more democratic. 

The following weekend, on January 17th BRDSA showed up to another protest, "Stop ICE Terror" organized by the [Party for Socialism and Liberation](https://pslweb.org/). Speakers from other local orgs such as [VOTE](https://www.voiceoftheexperienced.org/), [Indivisible BR](https://indivisiblebr.org/),  and   Baton Rouge DSA members volunteered to help provide security for the event, and treasurer Alex L delivered a passionate speech 


The federal government must rely on stoking fear and hatred within our communities because they know that cohesive, self-reliant communities are the greatest threat to its legitimacy. Why does DHS need a $115.6 billion budget if communities can come together to take care of themselves? Will giving unidentifiable masked men more guns and drones make the American people more free?

These tactics are not new. Our government claims to be a democracy, but it has a long history of using its monopoly on violence to disrupt labor organizers domestically and abroad. If enough workers band together and threaten shareholder profits, the past shows that [the police will find an excuse to shoot them](https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/americanexperience/features/theminewars-labor-wars-us/). And in a post 9/11 world, our national politics is increasingly driven by [the spectre of "national security"](https://www.kenklippenstein.com/p/trumps-nspm-7-labels-common-beliefs), rather than by a political view rooted in materially improving people's lives. 

Instead of building more housing, preparing for climate disaster, or providing care for the thousands of veterans left to rot on the streets, the federal government marches on in its never-ending quest for enemies within and for excuses to wage imperialist wars. If the federal government really wanted a secure nation, it would not suffer a child to know hunger or homelessness. It would not suffer an elder to wither away impoverished and alone. But that's not the kind of national security our government is interested in. Instead it wastes human lives on fruitless wars on drugs, wars on terror, and now wars on American cities. 

No, it wants the kind of national security that surveils, that redacts, that shoots first and refuses to investigate, that demands more tax dollars each year while refusing any kind of democratic oversight or accountability. Any group of people that does not want to participate in the exploitative and immoral game of capitalism are deemed threats to the security of the nation. Make no mistake, the federal government cares infinitely more about the bottom line of Chevron and Raytheon than it does about the ability of your family to keep a roof over your heads. They will uphold capitalism, no matter what it costs you, and no matter what it costs the planet.

BRDSA stands with workers and immigrants worldwide. We rise in solidarity with chapters nationwide in calling for an end to ICE and the reckless cruelty it has unleashed across this country. Every time our government ignores the will of the people, the cracks in its veneer of legitimacy deepen. "There is a crime here that goes beyond denunciation. There is a sorrow here that weeping cannot symbolize. There is a failure here that topples all our success. In the eyes of the people there is the failure; and in the eyes of the hungry there is a growing wrath."

Below are remarks delivered by Alex L, Treasurer of BRDSA.

> If I were reading a novel where a masked member of a secret police force killed a character whose last name was literally “Good”, I would probably be upset at the writers' lack of subtlety in symbolizing the moral stakes of the story. But, alas, this is not a work of fiction, this is sad, horrific reality. Which I suppose is just my fancy way of saying: the material, ethical, and political stakes of what's happening here are so clear, anyone who claims that they don't get it by now is lying to you. 
> 
> Not only that, but - since I started out by decrying the use of hackneyed metaphors, anyway - let's quit lying to ourselves & dispense with the Gestapo metaphors here. ‘Cause, comrades, we ain't in the Rhineland; we're in the Deep, American South. And if I bring up a group of masked men going door to door to catch POC - POC who are forced to do the agrarian labor that is the backbone of our entire economic system while simultaneously being considered less than US citizens - does it sound like I am talking about ICE agents or slave catchers now? Because we're in the United States of America. And if I bring up men who routinely detain & abuse non-English speaking immigrant-labors who are forced to do the work that builds this country's modern infrastructure at the whims of the richest men in the country - am I talking about ICE Agents now, or Pinkertons and other union busters in the Late 19th century? 
> 
> This situation has always been a part of American politics, but we don't even have to go back that far to see the connections. Because, if you look into the names on the contracts to build ICE's tools & weapons, and the ones on the contracts to build the IDF's, please don't be surprised when when you see the exact same names. But I digress. White supremacy and capitalism in this country have always been built on an underlying ideology of manifest destiny. But there is no frontier to chase Indigenous people across anymore. And even the targets in Southern Asia are running out, and so the front finally comes home; to the Americas; and in this case, to the United States of America.
> 
> And so I stand here now before you all and, with the most shattered of broken hearts, but the deepest sense of humility and reverence, invoke the name of Renee Nicole Macklin Good. But it's not just about her. There was Carlos Roberto Montoya Valdez, who died while attempting to escape an anti-immigrant raid at a Home Depot in California last August. There was Norlan Guzman-Fuentes, whose family this country's joke of a federal government Government could not even be bothered to notify after he lost his life in a mass shooting while being detained at an ICE facility in Dallas. And I could mention at least 30 other people who died in connection with ICE in the last year, too. 
> 
> But I'm only allotted so much time here. And so, I will close this by saying one final word. It is a word that the abjectly-privileged, Fauntleroy Plantation Owners & Robber Barons of the 19th century were as likely to find as alien then as Trump and all his billionaire cronies would today. And that word is: **No!** No, you will not get exactly what you want this time. The immigrant laborers in this country, their families and supporters, will not be defeated just to support your arbitrary whims. Because the arc of the moral universe may be very long, but it does not naturally bend toward justice. *It is bent*. It is pushed, and pulled, and hewn, and hammered, and tempered, and lifted, and greased with sweat, and with tears, and with blood. The blood of the workers. The blood of the immigrants. The blood of the people who don't fit whatever weird, gross definitions of Whiteness ruling class ideologies have foisted upon them this time. The blood of the people who actually understand what would happen if it were to bend toward injustice. And so, we stand here now in unwavering solidarity with the family & loved ones of Renee Good, and we say: 
> 
> Renee Good's body lies resting in her grave, but her truth is marching on
